What is Fair Value Gap (FVG)?

A three-candle imbalance where the middle candle's range doesn't overlap — price often returns to fill it.

The DefinitionFair Value Gap (FVG), defined

A Fair Value Gap (FVG) is a three-candle pattern where the middle candle moves so aggressively that its range doesn't overlap with the candles on either side — leaving a 'gap' between candle 1's high and candle 3's low (in an up move) or vice versa. The gap represents one-sided order flow, and price frequently returns to trade through ('fill') the gap before continuing. FVGs are high-probability Retrace zones: after a Reveal, the nearest unfilled FVG is often exactly where price pulls back to.

Why It MattersWhy Fair Value Gap (FVG) matters

FVGs are the market's footprints of urgency. Where price moved too fast to trade fairly, it tends to come back and make it fair.

In PracticeFair Value Gap (FVG) — a real example

Example · New York Session

Candle 1 high: 5,840. Candle 2 rips to 5,845. Candle 3 low: 5,842. The gap between 5,840 and 5,842 is the FVG. Price retraces into it 30 minutes later and bounces. Classic fill.

How This Fits the 6:00 AM Protocol
Stage 4 — The Retrace (7:00–8:00 AM)

A fair value gap is a Retrace magnet. When the Reveal creates an FVG, the Protocol says: wait for price to return and fill the gap before entering — that's your Retrace entry zone.
