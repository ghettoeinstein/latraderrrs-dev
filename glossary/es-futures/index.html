What is ES (E-mini S&P 500 Futures)?

The most liquid equity index futures contract in the world — the primary LA Traders instrument.

The DefinitionES (E-mini S&P 500 Futures), defined

ES is the E-mini S&P 500 futures contract traded on the CME, tracking the S&P 500 index at $50 per point per contract. It trades nearly 24 hours a day (Globex session), offers deep liquidity with 1-tick (0.25 point = $12.50) spreads, and is the most actively traded equity index future in the world. Its characteristics make it ideal for the RRR framework: clean technical levels, dense liquidity at obvious prices, and enough movement in the New York morning to offer multiple A+ setups per week.

Why It MattersWhy ES (E-mini S&P 500 Futures) matters

Liquidity is everything. On ES, your levels get respected because the whole world is watching them, and your fills are instant because the book is deep.
